# The Diabetes Risk Score: A practical tool to predict type 2 diabetes risk

**Background:** 
“Interventions to prevent type 2 diabetes should be directed toward individuals at increased risk for the disease. To identify such individuals without laboratory tests, we developed the Diabetes Risk Score.”[10.2337/diacare.26.3.725](https://diabetesjournals.org/care/article/26/3/725/29197/The-Diabetes-Risk-ScoreA-practical-tool-to-predict)

**Key Findings:**
“A random population sample of 35- to 64-year-old men and women with no antidiabetic drug treatment at baseline were followed for 10 years. New cases of drug-treated type 2 diabetes were ascertained from the National Drug Registry. Multivariate logistic regression model coefficients were used to assign each variable category a score. The Diabetes Risk Score was composed as the sum of these individual scores. The validity of the score was tested in an independent population survey performed in 1992 with prospective follow-up for 5 years.” [10.2337/diacare.26.3.725](https://diabetesjournals.org/care/article/26/3/725/29197/The-Diabetes-Risk-ScoreA-practical-tool-to-predict)

“Age, BMI, waist circumference, history of antihypertensive drug treatment and high blood glucose, physical activity, and daily consumption of fruits, berries, or vegetables were selected as categorical variables. Complete baseline risk data were found in 4,435 subjects with 182 incident cases of diabetes. The Diabetes Risk Score value varied from 0 to 20. To predict drug-treated diabetes, the score value ≥9 had sensitivity of 0.78 and 0.81, specificity of 0.77 and 0.76, and positive predictive value of 0.13 and 0.05 in the 1987 and 1992 cohorts, respectively.” [10.2337/diacare.26.3.725](https://diabetesjournals.org/care/article/26/3/725/29197/The-Diabetes-Risk-ScoreA-practical-tool-to-predict)

* “The ROC curves (Fig. 1) demonstrate that the Diabetes Risk Score based on the 1987 cohort predicted drug-treated diabetes very well (AUC = 0.85). The prediction was similarly good in the 1992 cohort (AUC = 0.87).” 
* “For cut point Diabetes Risk Score ≥9 (black marker), sensitivity was 0.78 (95% CI 0.71–0.84) and 0.81 (0.69–0.89), specificity was 0.77 (0.76–0.79) and 0.76 (0.74–0.77), PPV was 0.13 (0.11–0.15) and 0.05 (0.04–0.06), and negative predictive value was 0.99 (0.98–0.99) and 0.996 (0.993–0.998) in the 1987 and 1992 cohorts, respectively.” 
[10.2337/diacare.26.3.725](https://diabetesjournals.org/care/article/26/3/725/29197/The-Diabetes-Risk-ScoreA-practical-tool-to-predict)

**Calculator Integration:**
This foundational validation established the FINDRISC item set, scoring range (0–20), operating cut point (≥9), and diagnostic performance (AUC 0.85–0.87; sensitivity/specificity ~0.78–0.81/0.76–0.77), supporting use of FINDRISC for noninvasive risk stratification and referral to preventive interventions.
