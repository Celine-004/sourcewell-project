# Family History and Diabetes Risk Significance 

“Screening for prediabetes and type 2 diabetes risk through an assessment of risk factors (Table 2.5) or with an assessment tool, such as the American Diabetes Association risk test (Fig. 2.2), is recommended to guide whether to perform a diagnostic test for prediabetes (Table 2.2) and type 2 diabetes (Table 2.1) (see Section 2, ‘Diagnosis and Classification of Diabetes’).” [PMC10725807](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10725807/)

“To be eligible for this program, individuals must have a BMI in the overweight range and be at risk for diabetes based on laboratory testing, a previous diagnosis of GDM, or a positive risk test (cdc.gov/prediabetes/takethetest/).” [PMC10725807](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10725807/)

The American Diabetes Association (ADA) provides the following pharmacologic intervention recommendation (derived from the "Pharmacologic Interventions to Delay Symptomatic Type 1 Diabetes" section).The letter at the end of each statement represents the evidence grade:
* “Teplizumab has been approved to delay the onset of stage 3 type 1 diabetes in people 8 years of age and older with stage 2 type 1 diabetes based in part on the results of a single trial in relatives of people with type 1 diabetes (126). In this study, 44 individuals were randomized to a 14-day course of teplizumab and 32 to placebo. The median time to stage 3 type 1 diabetes diagnosis was 48.4 months in the teplizumab group and 24.4 months in the placebo group. Type 1 diabetes was diagnosed in 19 (43%) of participants who received teplizumab and 23 (72%) of those who received placebo (HR 0.41 [95% CI 0.22–0.78]).” [PMC10725807](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10725807/)

**Calculator Integration:**
Section 3 recommends using “an assessment of risk factors… or… a validated risk test” to guide testing, and program eligibility includes a “positive risk test,” which inherently weighs family history; this supports mapping the family history item within FINDRISC for risk-based screening and referral to preventive interventions.[PMC10725807](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10725807/)
