# BMI categories and diabetes risk relationship

The American Diabetes Association (ADA) provides the following pharmacologic intervention recommendation (derived from the "Pharmacologic Interventions" section).The letter at the end of each statement represents the evidence grade:
* “3.7 Metformin for the prevention of type 2 diabetes should be considered in adults at high risk of type 2 diabetes, as typified by the DPP, especially those aged 25–59 years with BMI ≥35 kg/m2, higher fasting plasma glucose (e.g., ≥110 mg/dL [≥6 mmol/L]), and higher A1C (e.g., ≥6.0% [≥42 mmol/mol]), and in individuals with prior gestational diabetes mellitus. A”
[PMC10725807](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10725807/)

The American Diabetes Association (ADA) provides the following recommendation for person-centered care (derived from the "Person-Centered Care Goals" section).The letter at the end of each statement represents the evidence grade:
* “3.14 More intensive preventive approaches should be considered in individuals who are at particularly high risk of progression to diabetes, including individuals with BMI ≥35 kg/m2, those at higher glucose levels (e.g., fasting plasma glucose 110–125 mg/dL [6.1–6.9 mmol/L], 2–h postchallenge glucose 173–199 mg/dL [9.6–11.0 mmol/L]), and A1C ≥6.0% [≥42 mmol/mol]), and individuals with a history of gestational diabetes mellitus. A”
[PMC10725807](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10725807/)

“To qualify for Medicare coverage, individuals must have BMI >25 kg/m2 (or BMI >23 kg/m2 if self-identified as Asian) and glycemic testing consistent with prediabetes in the last year.” [PMC10725807](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10725807/)

**Calculator Integration:**
FINDRISC includes BMI as a scored risk factor. ADA’s Section 3 identifies overweight/obesity thresholds relevant to risk stratification (e.g., BMI >25 kg/m2 or >23 kg/m2 for Asian individuals for program eligibility; BMI ≥35 kg/m2 as “particularly high risk”[PMC10725807](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10725807/)), which can be mapped to the BMI item in FINDRISC and used to prioritize more intensive preventive interventions for higher-risk categories.
